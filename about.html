Who built this weather platform

My name is Jeff Forgeron and I am a professional meteorologist with more than a decade of forecasting experience across multiple industries.

I began my career in broadcast television where I spent many years delivering daily weather forecasts to viewers across the Pacific Northwest. During that time I earned the American Meteorological Society’s Certified Broadcast Meteorologist (CBM) designation, one of the highest professional certifications in broadcast weather.

Today I work in the renewable energy sector as a Real-Time Trading Meteorologist for Avangrid. In this role, weather forecasts directly influence energy markets and grid operations. I provide hour-by-hour and day-ahead forecasts focused on wind and solar generation across the Columbia River Basin, helping energy traders understand how atmospheric conditions impact renewable power production.

In addition to forecasting, I build real-time data visualization tools that help monitor weather conditions and renewable energy output across multiple wind and solar assets.

This website was built as a personal project to make weather information easier to access and easier to interpret. My goal is to bring together the most useful weather tools — forecasts, observations, webcams, tides, and real-time conditions — into a single streamlined dashboard.

Weather affects everything from travel to outdoor recreation to energy markets, and I believe it should be easy for people to quickly understand what the atmosphere is doing.

This site will continue evolving as new tools, locations, and features are added over time.
